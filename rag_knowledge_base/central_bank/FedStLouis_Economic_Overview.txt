ROLE OF THE FEDERAL RESERVE SYSTEM:
The Federal Reserve (Fed) is the central bank of the United States. The Fed:
1. Sets monetary policy to promote:
   - Maximum employment
   - Stable prices (inflation target ~2%)
   - Moderate long-term interest rates
2. Promotes financial system stability
3. Supervises and regulates financial institutions
4. Fosters a safe, efficient and accessible payments system
5. Promotes consumer protection and community development

THE FEDERAL FUNDS RATE:
- The interest rate at which banks lend reserves to each other overnight
- Primary tool of monetary policy
- Affects interest rates throughout the economy (mortgage rates, savings rates, bond yields, etc.)
- When the Fed raises rates: borrowing becomes more expensive, can slow inflation; reduces bond prices
- When the Fed lowers rates: borrowing becomes cheaper, can stimulate economy; increases bond prices

FRED (Federal Reserve Economic Data):
- Free public database of economic time series
- URL: fred.stlouisfed.org
- Includes: interest rates, inflation, GDP, unemployment, stock indices, exchange rates, etc.
- Essential reference for investment research and portfolio analysis

IMPLICATIONS FOR INVESTORS:
- Interest rate environment heavily influences asset allocation decisions
- Rising rates → bonds decrease in value (existing bonds less attractive)
- Rising rates → may slow stock market growth (higher discount rates reduce valuations)
- Falling rates → bonds increase in value
- Inflation erodes real returns on fixed-income investments
- PCE inflation above the 2% target may signal Fed tightening to come
