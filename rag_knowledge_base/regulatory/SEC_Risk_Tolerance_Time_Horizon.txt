=== GAUGE YOUR RISK TOLERANCE ===

What are the best saving and investment products for you? The answer depends on:
1. When you will need the money (time horizon)
2. Your goals
3. Whether you will be able to sleep at night if you purchase a risky investment (one where you could lose your principal)

LONG TIME HORIZON (35 years to retirement):
- May want to consider riskier investment products
- Sticking to only "savings" products or less risky investments means money may grow too slowly
- Given inflation and taxes, may lose purchasing power of money
- COMMON MISTAKE: Putting money you will not need for a very long time in investments that pay a low amount of interest

SHORT TIME HORIZON (5 years or less):
- Don't choose risky investments
- When it's time to sell, you may have to take a loss if you're in volatile investments

KEY PRINCIPLE: Match investment risk to time horizon and goals.
- Short-term goals → lower-risk investments (preserve capital)
- Long-term goals → can accept higher risk for higher potential returns
- Consider impact of inflation on purchasing power over long periods

RISK TOLERANCE DIMENSIONS:
- Ability to take risk: Determined by time horizon, financial situation, income stability
- Willingness to take risk: Psychological comfort with potential losses

These two dimensions can diverge — someone may have a high ability but low willingness to take risk. Good financial advice addresses both.
