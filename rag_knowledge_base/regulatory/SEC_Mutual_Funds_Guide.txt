=== MUTUAL FUNDS ===

WHAT ARE MUTUAL FUNDS?
A mutual fund is an SEC-registered open-end investment company that pools money from many investors. It invests the money in stocks, bonds, short-term money-market instruments, other securities or assets, or some combination of these investments. The combined holdings the mutual fund owns are known as its portfolio, which is managed by an SEC-registered investment adviser. Each mutual fund share represents an investor's part ownership of the mutual fund's portfolio and the gains and losses the portfolio generates.

WHY DO PEOPLE BUY MUTUAL FUNDS?
- Professional Management: Managed by SEC-registered investment advisers.
- Diversification: May invest in a range of companies and industries, lowering risk.
- Low Minimum Investment: Many mutual funds set a relatively low dollar amount for initial investment.
- Liquidity: Investors can readily sell shares back to the fund at the next calculated NAV on any business day minus any redemption fees.

HOW DO INVESTORS EARN MONEY FROM MUTUAL FUNDS?
1. Dividend Payments: The fund earns income (dividends on stock, interest on bonds) and pays shareholders nearly all of it, less expenses.
2. Capital Gains Distributions: When a fund sells a security that has increased in price, it distributes these capital gains (minus losses) to investors at year end.
3. Increased Net Asset Value (NAV): If the market value of a fund's portfolio increases (after deducting expenses and liabilities), the NAV and share price increases.

RISKS OF MUTUAL FUNDS
Mutual funds are NOT guaranteed or insured by the FDIC or any other government agency. You may lose some or all of the money you invest. A fund's past performance does not predict future returns but can indicate volatility. Higher potential return generally means higher risk of loss.

TYPES OF MUTUAL FUNDS
- Stock funds: Invest primarily in stocks/equities. Can rise and fall quickly.
- Bond/income funds: Invest primarily in bonds or debt securities.
- Target date funds: Hold a mix of stock funds, bond funds and other funds; designed for individuals with a particular retirement date. Mix becomes more conservative as target date approaches.
- Money market funds: Invest in liquid, short-term debt securities, cash and cash equivalents.

INVESTING STRATEGIES
- Index Funds (Passive): Designed to achieve approximately the same return as a particular index before fees. Usually translates into less trading, lower costs, more favorable tax consequences.
- Actively Managed Funds: Seek to achieve stated investment objective by investing in a portfolio of stocks, bonds, and other assets. Potential to outperform market but heavily dependent on manager skill.

WHAT DO YOU PAY?
Funds pass costs to investors by deducting fees and expenses from NAV. Even small differences in fees can mean large differences in returns over time. A fund with high costs must perform better than a low-cost fund to generate the same returns.

KEY CONSIDERATIONS BEFORE INVESTING
- Read the fund's prospectus and most recent shareholder report
- Consider whether the fund fits your overall financial situation
- Ask about where your money is going, who is managing it, and how to get it back
