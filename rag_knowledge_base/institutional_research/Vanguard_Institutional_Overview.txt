=== VANGUARD INSTITUTIONAL — INVESTMENT PHILOSOPHY & RESOURCES ===

VANGUARD CORE PRINCIPLES (from CEO letter):
- Emphasis on low-cost, long-term investing
- Focus on investor outcomes, not short-term performance
- Diversification across asset classes

KEY PRODUCTS:
1. Mutual Funds, Trusts, and ETFs
2. Target Date Funds (TDFs)
3. Defined Contribution (DC) Plans / 401(k)
4. Nonprofit investing
5. Corporate accounts

TARGET DATE FUND PHILOSOPHY:
- Gradually shifts emphasis from more aggressive to more conservative investments as target date approaches
- Year in fund name = approximate retirement date
- Income Trust/Fund and Income and Growth Trust have fixed allocations for already-retired investors
- NOT guaranteed at any time, including on or after the target date

VANGUARD'S INVESTING PRINCIPLES (Principles for Investing Success):
1. Goals — Create clear, appropriate investment goals
2. Balance — Develop a suitable asset allocation using diversified funds
3. Cost — Minimize cost (expense ratios matter enormously over time)
4. Discipline — Maintain perspective and long-term discipline

VANGUARD ON ASSET ALLOCATION:
- Asset allocation is the primary driver of portfolio return variability
- Should reflect investor's time horizon, risk tolerance, and financial situation
- Younger investors: can afford more equity exposure due to longer recovery time
- Older investors: shift toward more conservative/income-oriented allocations

VANGUARD ON COSTS:
- Every dollar paid in fees is a dollar not compounding for the investor
- Low-cost index funds typically outperform most actively managed funds over long periods
- Expense ratios, transaction costs, and tax efficiency all matter

VANGUARD ON DISCIPLINE (Behavioral Coaching):
- Behavioral biases (e.g., panic selling, performance chasing) destroy wealth
- "Advisor's Alpha" research: up to ~1.5% annual value from behavioral coaching alone
- Staying invested during market downturns is essential to capturing long-term returns
